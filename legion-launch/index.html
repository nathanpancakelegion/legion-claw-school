The Opportunity

There's a window for AI-native founders. It won't be open long.

Most founders are still building businesses the way founders built them in 2019. Hire a team, set up the systems, then look at AI later. That's still a viable path, and it works.

But there's another path that didn't exist five years ago, and it changes the shape of what's possible. Build the business AI-native from day one. The architecture, the workflows, the org chart, the customer journey — designed around AI from the start.

An AI-native business runs leaner. It scales faster. It hits unit economics that founders building the old way can't replicate. It's available to anyone willing to start from scratch and build it that way deliberately.

That's what Legion Launch is. 30 days to take an idea from zero to an AI-native business that's incorporated, operating, and earning. The window is open right now. Walk through it before it closes.

The Solution

Five agents.
One org chart.

An AI workforce isn't one chatbot. It's five agents with roles, handoffs, and a clear org chart. They do the work. They escalate to you only when there's signal. We teach you to build the same thing for your business.

We teach you to build that org chart for your business — before you incorporate, before you hire, before you spend on the wrong tool.

FAQ

Straight answers.

How do I know this isn't a scam?+
Pressure-test it. Legion Claw School — the website you're reading right now — was vibe-coded by Nathan in roughly a week using the same agent stack the program teaches you to build. Both founders are public record: Nathan Pancake is a Marine veteran with a Georgetown master's in commercial real estate; Professor P. Simon Mahler is a professor of entrepreneurship at Texas A&M. We're in the room with you, live, five days a week. Everything you'd verify about this program is verifiable.
What if I'm building an inventory-heavy business — food service, retail, trades? Is 5% too much?+
That's exactly why we tier the rate. Inventory and material-heavy businesses pay 3%, not 5%. We agree on which tier you're in at the fit call before you enroll, and that rate is locked in your participant agreement. Read the full revenue share terms →
Why no cap or end date on the revenue share?+
It's an alignment payment, not a fee for service. The moment we cap or end the relationship, we stop being incentivized to help you in year 5 and year 10. We want to be your partner for the life of the business — that requires the structure to be permanent. More on why →
Is this a good fit if I already have a business?+
Honest answer: no. Legion Launch is built for founders starting from scratch — the 30-day curriculum walks you from idea to incorporated, operating, and earning. If you already have a running business, the program will fight your existing operations rather than fit them. If you have an existing business and want to build an AI workforce inside what you already have, the right product is Build Your AI Workforce — our self-paced course that teaches the same agent system without the cohort structure.
What happens after the 30 days?+
60 days of dedicated remote support — office hours, troubleshooting, accountability check-ins. Then you're a Legion graduate for the life of your business: continued community access, ongoing office hours, direct support from Legion's team and other graduates, indefinitely. Our revenue share runs for the life of your business; so does our partnership with you. That's what keeps us materially incentivized to pick up the phone in year 5.
What does the rev-share actually look like?+
A small percentage of your gross revenue — 3% for inventory-heavy businesses, 5% for everything else — paid only when your business earns. No upfront tuition. No equity. Tier locked at the fit call before you enroll. Read the full revenue share terms →
