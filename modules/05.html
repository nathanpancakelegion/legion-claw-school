Why Research Is the Highest-ROI Automation

Research is the silent time killer. Before a meeting, you spend 30 minutes googling a company. Before entering a market, you spend hours reading reports. Before a sales call, you're scanning LinkedIn profiles. Most of this research follows a pattern — same types of questions, same types of sources, same output format. That pattern is exactly what an AI agent excels at. A well-built research agent replaces 5-10 hours per week of manual research with 5-10 minutes of agent briefings. And the output is more consistent because the agent never skips steps.

Building Recon: Your Research Agent

If you built Recon in Module 04, we're going to upgrade it. Here's Recon's full Role Card. Role: "You are Recon, my research and intelligence analyst. You produce structured, actionable briefings. You are thorough but concise — every sentence should earn its place. You never pad output with filler. You always cite your reasoning and flag when you're making inferences vs. stating facts." Input: One of four research triggers — Person Brief (a name and context, produce a profile), Company Brief (a company name, produce a company analysis), Market Brief (an industry, geography, or trend, produce a market overview), or Competitive Brief (a competitor or set of competitors, produce a comparison analysis). Constraints: Clearly label facts vs. inferences, if you cannot find reliable information say so — do not fabricate, keep each brief to one page (400-600 words) unless asked for more depth, prioritize recent activity (last 12 months), and always end with "Implications for my business."

The Four Research Templates

Person Brief: Background (2-3 sentences on role, company, career trajectory), Relevant Activity (recent news, posts, talks, deals from last 12 months), Connection Points (what you have in common, mutual contacts, shared interests), Talking Points (3 personalized conversation starters), Watch-Outs (red flags, controversies, sensitivities), and Implications (why this person matters and what the play is). Company Brief: Overview (what they do, size, leadership), Business Model (how they make money, who their customers are), Recent Developments (last 12 months — funding, launches, partnerships), Strengths, Vulnerabilities, Alignment (partnership, competition, customer, or irrelevant), and Recommended Action. Market Brief: Market Overview (size, growth trajectory, key players), Key Trends (3-5 trends shaping the market now), Regulatory Landscape, Opportunity Assessment (where the gaps are), Risk Factors, and Implications. Competitive Brief: Competitor Profiles, Comparison Matrix, Where We Win, Where They Win (be honest), and Strategic Recommendations.

Upgrading Recon with Knowledge Files

Upload these to Recon's Claude Project: Your company one-pager — so Recon always knows who you are and what you do when writing the "Implications" section. Your current prospect/target list — so Recon can cross-reference and add context. An example of a good brief you've written or received — so Recon has a quality benchmark. Industry glossary (if your field has specialized terms) — so Recon uses correct terminology.

Real-World Workflow: The Morning Intel Briefing

Here's how I use Recon every day. Every morning, I give Recon a batch prompt: "Today I have meetings with Person A at Company X and Person B at Company Y. I'm also monitoring a market trend. Produce: Person Brief for Person A, Company Brief for Company X, Person Brief for Person B, Company Brief for Company Y, and a 3-sentence update on the market trend — just the latest, nothing I'd already know." In one prompt, I get my entire day's intelligence. Total time: 3 minutes to write the prompt, 2 minutes to review the output. That replaces an hour or more of manual research.

Pairing Recon with Perplexity

Recon works with the information in its context and training data. For real-time, sourced research, pair it with Perplexity. Use Perplexity to pull current data with citations (funding rounds, news, market stats). Paste Perplexity's output into Recon with: "Incorporate this into a Company Brief for this company." Recon structures and analyzes it within your framework. This gives you the best of both: real-time data from Perplexity, structured analysis from Recon.

Building Your Knowledge Base

Every brief Recon produces should be saved. Next time you meet with the same person or company, you start from the last brief instead of zero. Over time, you build a proprietary intelligence database. Your agents get smarter as they reference previous research. Save briefs in your Obsidian vault with a simple naming convention: People as Name-Brief.md, Companies as Company-Brief.md, Markets as Market-Brief.md.
