Module 07: Automating Sales & Outreach

The Sales Problem AI Actually Solves

Most people hear "AI for sales" and think "spam at scale." That's the opposite of what we're building. The real problem AI solves in sales isn't volume — it's personalization at volume. You know that the best outreach is personal, relevant, and specific. You also know that writing personalized emails for 50 prospects takes 10 hours. So you either send generic outreach (low response rates) or you personalize a few and neglect the rest. Closer solves this. It produces personalized, contextual outreach for every prospect — at the quality of your best manual emails, at the speed of a template.

Building Closer: Your Sales Agent

Here's Closer's full Role Card. Role: "You are Closer, my sales and outreach agent. You write like a smart, direct founder — not a salesperson. Your outreach feels like it's from a real person who did their homework, because it is. You understand that the goal of cold outreach is to start a conversation, not close a deal." Input: Prospect information (name, company, role, relevant background), research brief from Recon when available, outreach type (cold email, follow-up, LinkedIn message, warm intro request), and any specific angle or context. Constraints: Cold emails under 120 words, no exceptions. Follow-ups under 80 words. LinkedIn messages under 100 words. Never use "I hope this finds you well," "circling back," "just following up," "synergy," "leverage," or "value proposition." Always include one specific personal detail that proves you researched them. CTA is always low-commitment: 15-minute call, quick question, or asking for their take. Never pitch in the first email — open a door, don't push through it. Subject lines under 6 words, lowercase, no punctuation gimmicks.

The 4-Touch Outreach Sequence

Touch 1 — The Opener (Day 1): Establish relevance, show you did your homework, ask one low-friction question. One sentence referencing something specific about them, one sentence about what you do and why it's relevant (a connection point, not a pitch), and a low-commitment CTA. Touch 2 — The Value Add (Day 4): Give something useful, don't ask for anything. Share an article, a data point, or a market insight relevant to their work. Brief tie-back to why you shared it. No CTA this time. Touch 3 — The Direct Ask (Day 8): Clear, final ask. One sentence with a direct request for 15 minutes and specific suggested times. One sentence on what you'd cover and why it's worth their time. Offer to reconnect in the future if timing isn't right. Touch 4 — The Breakup (Day 14): Last touch, low pressure, door stays open. Acknowledge you've reached out a few times and don't want to be a pest. One sentence summary of why you reached out. No hard feelings if timing isn't right.

Personalizing at Scale with Recon + Closer

Here's the workflow that makes this system powerful. Build a prospect list: 20 names with company, role, and one line of context. Run each through Recon with "Quick Person Brief on this person at this company — focus on recent activity and alignment with your business." Feed each Recon brief into Closer with "Write Touch 1 for this prospect, here's the Recon brief." Review, edit minimally, send. For 20 prospects, this takes about an hour. Manually, it would take a full day.

Objection Handling Library

Closer can also prep you for conversations. Build an objection library: "Here are the 10 most common objections I get in sales conversations. For each, provide: why the prospect is really saying this (the underlying concern), a 2-3 sentence response framework, and a pivot question that moves the conversation forward." Upload this to Closer's project as a knowledge file. Before any meeting, ask: "Based on this prospect's profile, which objections are they most likely to raise? Prep me with responses."

LinkedIn Content as Outreach

Closer isn't just for direct messages. It can draft LinkedIn posts that serve as indirect outreach: "Write a LinkedIn post about a topic relevant to my target audience. The post should demonstrate expertise, share a specific insight or experience, and be the kind of thing my target prospects would find useful. Under 200 words. End with a question that invites engagement." Consistent LinkedIn content warms up your entire network. When you then send a direct message, the prospect has already seen your name and your thinking.

Follow-Up Cadence Management

The biggest leak in most sales processes isn't bad outreach — it's no follow-up. People send one email, get no response, and quit. Use Ops + Closer together: Ops tracks your outreach cadence — "Here's my current outreach log, which prospects are due for a follow-up this week, and what touch number are they on?" Closer drafts the appropriate follow-up for each — "Draft Touch 2 for this person at this company, here's the context from our initial outreach." You review, send, and log the activity back in Ops. This system ensures no prospect slips through the cracks.

What Closer Should Never Do

Be clear about the boundaries. Closer drafts — you send. Always review before anything goes out. Closer personalizes — you authenticate. Make sure the personal details are accurate. Closer suggests — you decide. If a prospect isn't a fit, Closer's job is to tell you that, not to pitch them anyway. Closer doesn't handle inbound — for responding to incoming inquiries, that's Ghost's domain with a different set of instructions.
